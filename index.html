Acute Stroke Management Guide

Telestroke Quick Reference — IV TNK • Extended Window • EVT

Code Stroke Activation Criteria

LKW ≤ 4.5 Hours

Activate code stroke if NOT suspected peripheral etiology and any of:

  • SUDDEN trouble walking, loss of balance or coordination
  • SUDDEN trouble seeing in one or both eyes
  • SUDDEN numbness or weakness on one side of the body
  • SUDDEN confusion, trouble speaking or understanding
  • SUDDEN severe headache with no known cause + neuro symptoms
  • SUDDEN, unexplained comatose-like state

LKW >4.5 – 24 Hours

  • Sudden, unexplained comatose-like state, OR
  • FANG-D positive (see FANG-D tab)

LKW >24 Hours or Resolved Symptoms

No code stroke. Non-emergent imaging only. No CT perfusion role.

Clinical Pearl
NIHSS ≥6 generally captures disabling deficits, but specific deficits (isolated hemianopsia, severe aphasia) may be disabling even at NIHSS <6.

NOT Clearly Disabling (IVT may not benefit)

  • Isolated facial droop
  • Hemisensory loss only
  • Mild cortical hand weakness (esp. nondominant)
  • Mild hemimotor or hemisensorimotor loss
  • Mild hemiataxia (still ambulatory)
  • Isolated mild aphasia (still communicates meaningfully)

Can the patient do ADLs (Bathing, Ambulating, Toileting, Hygiene, Eating) if deficits persist?

IV TNK Within 4.5 Hours (AHA 2026 Class I)

Eligibility

  • Age ≥ 18 years
  • Measurable, disabling neurologic deficit
  • Symptom onset (or LKW) ≤ 4.5 hours
  • No hemorrhage on NCCT
  • No contraindications (see TNK Contraindications tab)
AHA 2026 Key Update
Tenecteplase 0.25 mg/kg (max 25 mg) or alteplase 0.9 mg/kg is recommended (Class I). Initiate ASAP. Do NOT delay for CTA/CTP or MRI within 4.5h — NCCT alone is sufficient.

Examples of Clearly Disabling Deficits (IVT Indicated)

  • NIHSS ≥ 6
  • Complete hemianopsia
  • Severe aphasia (inability to communicate meaningfully)
  • Severe hemi-attention or extinction to >1 modality (neglect)
  • Forced eye deviation or gaze preference
  • Any weakness limiting sustained effort against gravity

Examples of NOT Clearly Disabling Deficits (IVT May NOT Benefit)

Class 3: No Benefit for IVT
For non-disabling deficits, trials failed to show IVT benefit. Dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT) is preferred.
  • Isolated facial droop
  • Hemisensory loss only
  • Mild cortical hand weakness (esp. nondominant, NIHSS 0 in motor)
  • Mild hemimotor or hemisensorimotor loss
  • Mild hemiataxia (still ambulatory)
  • Isolated mild aphasia (still communicates meaningfully)

Can the patient do ADLs (Bathing, Ambulating, Toileting, Hygiene, Eating) if deficits persist?

Key Principle
When in doubt — weigh severity and disability of the presenting stroke against bleeding risk. For severe, clearly disabling deficits, IVT benefit generally outweighs risk. Consultation with stroke neurology is recommended.

Extended Window IV TNK: Non-LVO / MeVO / DVO

4.5–24 hours • Based on OPTION trial • Limited to FANG-D positive patients

OPTION Trial (JAMA 2026)
IV TNK 0.25 mg/kg in non-LVO with salvageable tissue: mRS 0–1 at 90d: 43.6% vs 34.2% (RR 1.28, P=0.02, NNT=11). sICH: 2.8% vs 0%.

Inclusion Criteria

  • Age ≥ 18
  • FANG-D positive (local requirement)
  • NIHSS 6–25 (or 4–5 with disabling deficit)
  • Pre-stroke mRS 0 or 1
  • LKW 4.5 to 24 hours
  • Qualifying vessel: M2, M3-M4, ACA, PCA, stenosis, or vertebrobasilar occlusion not eligible for EVT
  • CTP mismatch: core <50 mL, ratio ≥1.2, mismatch vol ≥10 mL

Exclusion Criteria

  • Extensive hypodensity on NCCT (clear hypodensity responsible for symptoms — density greater than contralateral unaffected white matter)
  • NCCT hypodensity larger than CTP-defined ischemic core (mismatch inconsistency — significantly higher sICH risk)
  • LVO present (ICA, M1, or vertebrobasilar) AND planned EVT
  • Pre-stroke mRS > 1
  • No CTP mismatch
  • FANG-D negative (current local restriction)
Important
All absolute and relative contraindications for IV TNK within the standard 4.5-hour window (see TNK Contraindications tab) also apply to extended-window treatment. Review before administering.
Safety Note
sICH: 2.8% vs 0%. Two of 21 patients with NCCT hypodensity larger than perfusion-defined core developed sICH. Carefully evaluate NCCT alongside perfusion.
If CTP Not Available or Technically Limited
STAT MRI brain with DWI-FLAIR mismatch can be considered to determine eligibility: presence of DWI lesion with absence of corresponding FLAIR lesion indicates salvageable tissue.

Extended Window IV TNK: LVO (4.5–24h)

For patients NOT eligible for EVT or anticipated delay ≥60–90 min

Evidence
TRACE-III: TNK in LVO w/o EVT → mRS 0–1: 33% vs 24.2% (RR 1.37, P=0.03)
HOPE: Alteplase, no EVT → mRS 0–1: 40.3% vs 26.3% (aRR 1.52, P=0.004)
AHA 2026: Class 2b

When to Consider

  • NOT a candidate for EVT
  • Anticipated delay to groin puncture ≥ 60–90 min
  • At spoke/PSC without thrombectomy + prolonged transfer

Inclusion Criteria

  • Age ≥ 18
  • LVO: ICA, M1, M2 (TRACE-III); any occlusion (HOPE)
  • NIHSS 6–25 (TRACE-III) or 4–26 (HOPE)
  • Pre-stroke mRS 0 or 1
  • LKW 4.5 to 24 hours
  • CTP mismatch: core <70 mL, ratio ≥1.8 (TRACE-III) or ≥1.2 (HOPE), mismatch vol ≥15 or ≥10 mL
  • EVT NOT planned or delay ≥60–90 min

Do NOT Give Extended-Window IVT If:

  • EVT immediately available (within ~60 min)
  • Core ≥ 70 mL
  • No perfusion mismatch
  • Pre-stroke mRS ≥ 2
  • Standard IVT contraindications
  • Large NCCT hypodensity exceeding CTP core
Priority
If eligible for BOTH extended-window IVT AND EVT, EVT takes priority. IVT is bridging/standalone only when EVT unavailable or significantly delayed.

Post-Thrombolysis Monitoring

  • Admit to ICU or stroke unit
  • BP + neuro checks: q15 min × 2h → q30 min × 6h → q1h until 24h
  • Keep SBP <180, DBP <105 post-IVT
  • Do NOT target SBP <140 (Class 3: No Benefit)
  • Delay NG tubes, Foley, arterial lines if safely manageable
  • If severe HA, acute HTN, N/V, or worsening exam → STOP infusion → STAT CT head
  • Follow-up CT/MRI at 24 hours before starting antiplatelets/anticoagulants

Meta-Analysis (Liu et al., Neurology 2025)
Across 6 large-core RCTs (n=1,887): EVT improved ordinal mRS (gOR 1.6, 95% CI 1.4–1.8), independent ambulation (mRS 0–3: RR 1.9), and reduced mortality (RR 0.9) despite higher sICH (RR 1.7). Benefit decreased with longer onset-to-treatment time.

STAT Neurologic Consult Criteria
Conditions requiring urgent neurology consultation regardless of stroke pathway
  1. Large hemispheric stroke with possibility of swelling
    • Higher concern in patients < 60 years
  2. Stroke symptoms with onset outside of 24-hour window AND acute decline in mental status
  3. Multiple seizures and/or possible Status epilepticus without improvement of mental status within 30 minutes
    • Not following commands
    • No withdrawal to painful stimulus
  4. Acute, unexplained change in mental status without return to baseline
  5. Potential acute non-traumatic spinal cord pathology
  6. Neuromuscular emergencies and/or neurological disorders causing impaired breathing
  7. Newly diagnosed intracranial venous/sinus thrombosis
